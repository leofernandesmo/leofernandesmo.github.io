About

I am a Professor and Researcher in Software Engineering at the Federal Institute of Alagoas (IFAL), Brazil.

My work focuses on building trustworthy software systems through software testing, automated reasoning, software security, and artificial intelligence.

Over the years, I have investigated topics such as automated test generation, fuzz testing, software quality assessment, formal reasoning techniques, and empirical methods for evaluating software engineering practices.

I am particularly interested in combining rigorous engineering methods with practical solutions that can be adopted by both researchers and practitioners.

Research and Innovation

In addition to academic research, I actively participate in innovation projects developed in collaboration with companies, government agencies, and public institutions.

These initiatives allow me to connect scientific research with real-world challenges, contributing to the development of technologies, processes, and solutions that generate practical impact.

CPSoftware

I lead the CPSoftware (Centro de Pesquisa em Software), a research center dedicated to advancing software engineering research, innovation, education, and technology transfer.

Through CPSoftware, I collaborate with students, researchers, industry partners, and public organizations on projects involving software quality, security, artificial intelligence, and emerging software technologies.

Research Philosophy

I believe that impactful software engineering research should combine scientific rigor with practical relevance.

For this reason, my work emphasizes empirical evaluation, reproducibility, open-source software, research artifacts, industry collaboration, and technology transfer.

Whenever possible, research outcomes should extend beyond publications to include software tools, datasets, educational resources, and innovations that can be adopted in real-world settings.
