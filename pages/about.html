About

Leonardo Fernandes is an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science at the Federal Institute of Alagoas (IFAL) , Brazil. He holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science (2020) and an M.Sc. in Computer Science (2012) from the Federal University of Pernambuco (UFPE), as well as a degree in Information Technology from IFAL (2005).

He leads CPSoftware (Centro de Pesquisa em Software) , a research group officially registered with the Brazilian National Council for Scientific and Technological Development (CNPq). CPSoftware conducts research in software quality assurance, software security, artificial intelligence, data science, and software engineering tools and techniques.

His research focuses on Software Testing, Mutation Testing, Automated Reasoning, Software Security, Fuzzing, AI for Software Engineering, and Software Engineering for AI. His work has been published in leading journals and conferences in Software Engineering, including Information and Software Technology, Journal of Systems and Software, Science of Computer Programming, International Conference on Software Testing, Verification and Validation (ICST), and Evaluation and Assessment in Software Engineering (EASE). His doctoral research on reducing the cost of mutation testing received the Best Paper Award at the Brazilian Symposium on Software Engineering (SBES).

Beyond academic research, Leonardo actively collaborates with industry and public-sector organizations through research, development, and innovation projects. Under his leadership, CPSoftware has established partnerships with companies and government institutions to develop technological solutions and professional training programs in areas such as Artificial Intelligence, Software Engineering, and 5G Networks. These initiatives have also resulted in software registrations and technology transfer activities.

Before joining academia full-time, he worked in the software industry between 2005 and 2010, contributing to software analysis, architecture, design, implementation, testing, deployment, and maintenance projects.
